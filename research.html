Impact Cratering
I am interested in both the impact cratering process and using craters to understand the physical properties of projectile and the surface. My past research has included looking at the geometry of craters on Mercury to see what it can tell us about the strength of Mercury's crust and if impact velocity can play a role in final crater morphometry. In the future I plan to investigate how projectiles interact the suface in the contact stage of impact cratering using both experimental and computer simulations.
Surface Roughness
My ongoing work involves invetsigating the surface roughness of Mercury. This work is ongoing.
Laser Altimetry
My research involves looking at data derived from laser altimeters to get topography and surface roughness information.
